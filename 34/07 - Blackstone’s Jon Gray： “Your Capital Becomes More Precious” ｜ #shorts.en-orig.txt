The number one thing about a period of
dislocation as it relates to private
equity is there's a repricing that often
happens. There may be a company on the
screen that you were looking to
privatize. Now it's trading 15% cheaper
than it was before. So buying it becomes
more attractive. At the same time, there
are participants who need to raise
liquidity and so they need certainty to
execute a sale and so your capital
becomes more precious.
