- People need to think about
private credit differently.
Not a day goes by that we're not asked
about whether or not private
credit represents a bubble,
and I would just say those
fears tend to be misguided.
We see a long-term secular shift.
The model of private credit
is actually structured
to reduce risk in financial markets.
We operate with low levels of leverage,
and ultimately, bring together
long-term owners of assets
with long-term financing
needs for companies.
And we think that makes the
financial system more resilient.
One example was during
the banking crisis in 2023
when issuance volumes
dropped in the public markets
and private credit provided a solution.
We've been in private
credit for over 20 years,
which included periods of near-zero rates,
fast-increasing rates,
and then rates staying higher for longer.
Today, the credit
environment is very different
than what it was in 2008.
We finance larger companies
in better industries
at lower levels of leverage
than we did back then.
In 2016, I think you saw one
private credit transaction
that was over a billion dollars.
Over the last four years,
you've probably seen over 60 transactions
that were over a billion dollars
in first lien senior unitranche.
Today, we finance really
substantial businesses
at lower LTVs than we've ever seen,
so the opportunity set is much broader.
We're starting to see M&A
activity pick up across the board,
which ultimately translates in
higher amounts of deal supply
for private credit, as those
buyers need more financing
for the companies they acquire.
We're focused on sectors that we believe
have strong tailwinds in
this economic environment.
We tend to like software as
a service, business services
or healthcare, and we believe the pipeline
will continue to materialize.
This is more than just a moment in time.
We couldn't be more excited
about the opportunity
set we see ahead.
