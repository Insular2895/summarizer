- We're in a world today
where higher interest rates
and inflation have led to a
lot of economic volatility,
and frankly, questions about
the state of the economy.
In contrast to that,
though, what we're seeing
in the real estate space
is that fundamentals,
particularly in our core
sectors, are really the strongest
that they've been for quite some time.
And one of the reasons for
that is that in this cycle,
unlike prior cycles, we
didn't see excess lending,
and we also didn't see
excess levels of building.
And when you have less
construction and less supply
that's better for fundamentals.
That means stronger rents
and stronger occupancy.
What you want to own in an
inflationary environment
is hard assets with short duration leases
that have pricing power
in sectors with really good
supply demand fundamentals.
The thing you also want to do
is avoid investing in assets
that look and feel like
long duration bonds.
Think about an office building
that has a 20 year lease
with no growth.
That type of asset won't fare so well
in a higher rate environment.
You also want to avoid investing
in sectors that face structural headwinds.
Think about a regional
mall that's being impacted
by e-commerce.
Those are the types of places
you want to be much more cautious.
We've been saying for quite
some time that inflation
and interest rates are going
to be higher than consensus.
In order to produce value appreciation
you need to have cash flow growth
that can more than offset the impact
of lower valuation multiples
in a higher rate environment.
And today, around 80%
of our global portfolio
is in only four asset classes,
warehouses, rental, housing,
life science office, and in hotels.
All places that are
experiencing growth today
that is much higher than inflation.
That to us, is where you want to invest
in a moment like this.
